### What changed

- Increasing max sequence length from 512 to 1024 removed the remaining strict cosine failure on the selected legal gate.
- The worst remaining document cosine is `0.99989883`, above the `0.999` threshold.
- Ranking metrics are unchanged from the strong 512 result and remain excellent:

### What this proves

- The previous M015/M017 failures were sequence-length quality failures, not model replacement failures.
- For the selected 44-ФЗ gate, tagged Go ONNX with the native HF tokenizer and max sequence length 1024 can satisfy strict TEI-vs-ONNX quality thresholds.
- Deterministic chunking is not the immediate blocker for this selected corpus gate.

### What this does not prove

- It does not prove production readiness.
- It does not prove acceptable latency, memory use, throughput, Docker packaging, CI reproducibility, or artifact distribution.
- It does not prove quality for legal fragments longer than 1024 tokens.
- It does not justify switching the production/default runtime away from TEI.

## Recommended Next Gate

The next milestone should validate ONNX 1024 as an experimental runtime candidate, not promote it.

Recommended sequence:

1. Update tracked metadata/manifests or add a 1024-specific manifest so the sequence-length contract is explicit.

Chunking remains a future policy for unbounded legal text, but it is no longer the next immediate blocker for this selected gate.
