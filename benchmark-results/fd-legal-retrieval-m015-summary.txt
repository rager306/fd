## Interpretation

Ranking parity is mostly strong, but vector equivalence has severe outliers. The worst document cosine is `0.369489` and the worst query cosine is `0.656612`, far below the strict `0.999` equivalence threshold used for ONNX acceptance.

The worst examples are longer legal clauses/subclauses by ID and hash in the full artifact. This is consistent with a likely long-text/truncation divergence: the current ONNX export/runtime path uses max sequence length 128, while the legal corpus contains many longer fragments.

## Decision Impact

- TEI remains production/default.
- ONNX packaging/tuning should not be the next priority.
- Next work should investigate long-text legal quality divergence: chunking policy, max sequence length, TEI tokenization/truncation behavior, and possibly a longer-sequence ONNX export.

## Caveats

- The corpus has no explicit human qrels; this is a TEI-vs-ONNX parity and synthetic known-item gate.
- Raw legal text is intentionally excluded from this summary and from the full artifact.
- Full artifact: `benchmark-results/fd-legal-retrieval-m015-s03.txt`.
